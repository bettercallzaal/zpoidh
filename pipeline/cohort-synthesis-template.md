# Cohort Synthesis Template

After evaluating all submissions, analyze patterns across the full cohort. This is where Fable's value over human judges shines - it reads all submissions at once and spots patterns.

---

## Template

```
# [Round Title] - Cohort Synthesis

## Overview
- **Round:** [e.g., R2]
- **Total Submissions:** [N]
- **Floor-Pass Rate:** [N/total] ([%])
- **Score Range:** [min] - [max] (mean: [avg])
- **Winner:** [Display Name] ([score])

## Performance Distribution

### By Dimension
```
[Dimension Name]
- Mean Score: X.X/10
- Median Score: X.X/10
- Standout (high): [Submission] (score)
- Weakness (low): [Submission] (score)
- Interpretation: [What this means - e.g., cohort is strong on craft but weak on attribution]

[Repeat for each dimension]
```

### Score Quartiles
- **Top Quartile (>75th percentile):** [N submissions, scores X-Y]

## Filled Example: R2 POIDH Ad Cohort Synthesis

```
# R2: Best 60s POIDH Ad (Ep 19 w/ Kenny) - Cohort Synthesis

## Overview
- **Round:** 2
- **Total Submissions:** 7 floor-pass, 8 total (1 duration fail)
- **Floor-Pass Rate:** 7/8 (87.5%)
- **Score Range:** 5.2 - 8.95 (mean: 6.8)
- **Winner:** Monksage / joeyofdeus (8.95)

## Performance Distribution

### By Dimension

**Distribution (Weight: 25%)**
- Mean Score: 7.1/10
- Median Score: 7.0/10
- Standout (high): Monksage (9) - all three tags + team cross-tag + poidh.xyz
- Weakness (low): Kaspa (7) - basic tags, no cross-post
- Interpretation: Most submitters understood platform strategy (tags + links) but only top scorers nailed the full amplification picture (Farcaster + cross-tagging). This dimension is well-tuned to the brief.

**Craft (Weight: 25%)**
- Mean Score: 6.9/10
- Median Score: 7.0/10
- Standout (high): Monksage (8) - title cards match copy, optimized 59.7s, intentional cuts
- Weakness (low): [Submission X] (5) - looks like a slide show, drags at midpoint
- Interpretation: Cohort shows strong editing intent (no raw screencasts), but quality of pacing + hooks varies. Winners tightened their narrative arc; losers felt rambling even at 60s.

**Substance (Weight: 35%, highest importance)**
- Mean Score: 6.2/10
- Median Score: 6.0/10
- Standout (high): Monksage (10) - names Ep 19 + Kenny, hits permissionless-coordination thesis
- Weakness (low): [Submission Y] (4) - generic crypto ad, no Episode reference
- Interpretation: This is where winners separated from non-winners. Most submissions showed understanding of POIDH mission, but only top scorer *attributed the source* (Ep 19 + Kenny). This signals authenticity over generic ad copy.

**Bonus (Weight: 15%)**
- Mean Score: 5.8/10
- Median Score: 5.5/10
- Standout (high): Monksage (8) - question-answer copywriting hook + cross-tag strategy
- Weakness (low): [Multiple] (3-4) - straightforward execution, no creative spin
- Interpretation: Cohort largely played it safe. Winner showed copywriting craft (question framing). Next round: encourage "add a unique angle" messaging in the brief.

## Score Quartiles
- **Top Quartile (>75th percentile, >8.0):** 1 submission (Monksage, 8.95)
- **Upper-Middle (6.5-8.0):** 3 submissions (scores 7.2, 7.0, 6.8)
- **Lower-Middle (5.5-6.5):** 2 submissions (scores 6.2, 5.8)
- **Bottom Quartile (<5.5):** 2 submissions (scores 5.2, 4.9)

## Patterns in Winners (Top 3 Scorers)

**Pattern 1: Explicit Episode Attribution**
- All top 3 mention Ep 19 + Kenny by name (or strong reference)
- Example: Monksage - "BCZ YapZ Ep 19, @kennyistyping explains"
- Why: Shows submitter actually engaged with source material, not just guessed based on brief. Reads authentic.

**Pattern 2: Full Platform Amplification**
- Top 3 all tag all required handles + link poidh.xyz
- Top 3 have Farcaster cross-post or clear cross-post intent
- Why: Demonstrates understanding that reach = part of the scoring. Submitter invested in amplification, not just production.

**Pattern 3: Narrative Arc (Question → Answer)**
- Winner uses question-answer structure ("How do we...?" → "On Ep 19, X explains...")
- Other strong scorers use statement + supporting proof
- Why: Question engages viewer, answer delivers. Creates hook + payoff.

**Pattern 4: Optimized Duration**
- Winner at 59.7s (fully uses window, not squeezed)
- Other strong scorers at 59.7s and 60.1s
- Why: Shows technical craft, not just content. Indicates intentional editing, not "just under 60s".

## Gaps in Non-Winners

**Gap 1: No Episode Attribution (Weight 35% in Substance dimension)**
- Lower scorers post valid content but don't mention Ep 19 / Kenny
- Example: [Submission Z] scored 5.2 because it addressed adoption (true POIDH topic) but didn't tie to this specific episode
- How winners solved it: Monksage + top 2 all cite the episode by name in copy or video text
- Recommendation: In next round brief, emphasize "attribute the source episode" as a requirement, not optional bonus

**Gap 2: Partial Tag Set (Weight 25% in Distribution dimension)**
- Most non-winners tagged @bettercallzaal + @kennyistyping but missed @poidhxyz or poidh.xyz link
- Example: 4/8 submissions missing the full 3-tag set
- How winners solved it: Monksage tagged all three + cross-tagged the POIDH team itself
- Recommendation: Make the tag checklist more explicit in the brief ("Tag all three: @bettercallzaal, @kennyistyping, @poidhxyz + include poidh.xyz link")

**Gap 3: No Farcaster Cross-Post (Distribution dimension)**
- 5/8 submissions X-only
- Only 3/8 show visible Farcaster cross-post
- How winners solved it: Monksage implied strong cross-platform strategy; other strong scorers showed explicit cross-post links
- Recommendation: Include "cross-post to Farcaster /poidh channel" as explicit floor rule for next round (not just "helpful")

**Gap 4: Generic Messaging (Substance + Bonus dimensions)**
- Lower scorers use templates or generic crypto angles ("adoption", "decentralization")
- Winners use specific, episode-grounded messaging
- How winners solved it: Monksage framed as "response to Ep 19's specific question", not generic positioning
- Recommendation: Add rubric anchor: "Excellent = directly responds to the episode's central question or claim, not generic crypto positioning"

## Standout Submissions (Not Necessarily Winners)

- **[Submission B - Handle]:** Scored 7.2 but offers unique angle on [topic]. Strong craft, weaker substance tie-in. Worth elevating if next round emphasizes [dimension].

## Recommendations for Next Round

1. **Rubric Refinement:** Add "Excellent = directly attributes episode by name (not 'the podcast') + cites speaker name" to Substance anchor. Current 10-anchor was too loose.

2. **Brief Messaging:** Rewrite the brief to emphasize three things top scorers all did:
   - Tag all three handles + link poidh.xyz
   - Mention Ep 19 and Kenny by name in copy or video text
   - Show distribution intent (Farcaster cross-post or linked)

3. **Floor Rules:** Move "Cross-post to Farcaster /poidh" from nice-to-have to mandatory floor check (like tagging). Right now 5/8 missed it.

4. **Process Improvement:** In R3, run the evaluation on the first 3 days' submissions (not wait for deadline), so Zaal can post a reminder: "Reminder: mention Ep 19 + tag all three handles for higher scoring."

## Data Quality Flags

- **Ambiguity in Craft dimension:** Is "edited" strictly cuts-only or do title cards count? Monksage used title cards (intentional, strong) but Craft score jumped to 8 partly because of this. Clarify: "Excellent = intentional visual edits (cuts, transitions, on-screen text). Title cards alone = 6/10."

- **Rubric anchor for Bonus felt forced:** Why is question-framing a "bonus"? It's arguably fundamental to ad craft. Consider moving from Bonus (15%) to Craft (25%), and refactor Bonus to "unexpected creative elements" only.

- **Surprise:** Engagement (likes/replies) didn't correlate with scores as much as I expected. Monksage = 9 likes, but [Submission C] = 7 likes and scored lower only on dimension scores (not engagement). Rubric is working - measuring craft, not popularity. Good signal.

## Conclusion

The winner (Monksage, 8.95) clearly separated from the cohort by doing three things:
1. Named the source episode + Kenny (authenticity)
2. Tagged the full set + cross-tagged the team (strategy)
3. Used a question-answer hook (copywriting craft)

This round's rubric predicted a clear winner. Next round, emphasize these three mechanics in the brief so the floor-pass rate and overall quality lift.

## Key Insights Cohort Synthesis Should Surface

1. **What made the winner different?** (Don't just say "better score" - what specific behaviors?)
2. **Did the rubric predict quality?** (Did high scorers match judges' intuition?)
3. **What patterns did top submitters share?** (Actionable for briefing next round)
4. **What gaps did non-winners have?** (Rubric opportunity or messaging opportunity?)
5. **Rubric calibration:** (Does the anchor for "9/10" feel right, or too loose/strict?)
6. **Next-round messaging:** (One sentence on how to brief R[N+1] based on this cohort's wins + failures)

---

## Length

Aim for 1-2 pages. Cohort synthesis is not a full audit - it's a highlight reel of patterns + one recommendation per insight.

---

## Who Reads This

- **Judges:** Validate that the rubric is working (did top scorers match their intuition?)
- **Zaal:** Plan next round's brief + judge calibration
- **Submitters:** Show the cohort-level data (what made winners different?) to improve re-submission rate
- **Product:** Metric that Fable's scoring is stable + predictive (rubric reuse across rounds)
